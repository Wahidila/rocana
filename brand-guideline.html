01 — Brand Story

Capturing Souls in
the Island of Gods

Rocana.Studio is a Bali-based creative photography studio that captures the raw, authentic beauty of human emotion against the backdrop of nature's most breathtaking canvas.

Our Philosophy

The name "Rocana" draws from the Sanskrit word meaning "bright, radiant, illuminating." We believe every soul carries its own light — our craft is to reveal it through the lens, framed by the organic beauty of Bali's landscapes.

Our visual identity reflects the Wabi-sabi philosophy: finding beauty in imperfection. The spiral logomark, with its deliberately rough edges, embodies the handmade, authentic quality that defines our work. It is not polished to perfection — it is alive, organic, and deeply human.

We serve international clients — expats, destination wedding couples, luxury brands, and creative agencies — who seek photography that tells stories rather than simply captures moments.

02 — Logo System

Four Marks,
One Identity

The Rocana.Studio identity is built on a flexible system of four logo variants, each designed for specific contexts. Together they form a cohesive visual language that adapts from large-format prints to tiny social media avatars.

Logo Anatomy

Logomark (Spiral Symbol)

An organic spiral circle resembling a water vortex, fingerprint, or natural element. The letter "R" is seamlessly integrated into the center of the spiral. The deliberately rough, imperfect outer edges convey a handmade, earthy, and authentic quality — evoking the Wabi-sabi aesthetic prized by international clients. The spiral flows inward, symbolizing the journey of capturing the soul within.

Logotype (Wordmark)

Set in a bold, rounded sans-serif typeface (Nunito Black), the logotype "ROCANA.STUDIO" is warm yet authoritative. The rounded letterforms echo the organic curves of the spiral symbol, creating visual harmony between mark and type. The period between "ROCANA" and "STUDIO" acts as a deliberate pause — a breath between the brand name and its craft.

Clear Space
x x x = width of the "R" in the logomark

Always maintain a minimum clear space around any logo variant equal to the width of the letter "R" within the spiral logomark. This ensures the logo remains prominent and uncluttered in all applications. No other graphic elements, text, or edges should intrude into this protected zone.

Do
  • Use the correct variant for each context (see usage table)
  • Maintain the required clear space at all times
  • Use the inverted (white) version on dark or busy backgrounds
  • Use Logo Black for monochrome or high-contrast needs
  • Use Logo Mark alone for small sizes (avatars, favicons)
  • Use Logotype alone for horizontal, compact layouts
  • Keep all logo files in their original proportions
Don't
  • Stretch, skew, or distort any logo variant
  • Change the logo colors outside the approved palette
  • Add drop shadows, outlines, glows, or effects
  • Place the logo on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
  • Rotate the logo or rearrange the symbol and text
  • Recreate the logotype using alternative fonts
  • Combine the Logo Mark with a different typeface for the wordmark
  • Use the full Logo Main at sizes below the minimum threshold

Usage Guidelines

Recommended Combinations

Sage Olive logo on Ivory White or Sand Cream backgrounds for the primary brand look.

Obsidian Black text on light backgrounds for maximum readability.

Deep Moss for secondary text and supporting UI elements.

Muted Gold sparingly for luxury touches in print materials and foil stamping.

Inverted (white) logo on Obsidian Black for dark-mode and cinematic contexts.

Avoid

Never use Sage Olive as a background for body text — contrast is insufficient.

Avoid combining Warm Stone text on Sand Cream — too low contrast.

Do not introduce bright, saturated colors outside this palette.

Never use gradients that mix brand colors with non-palette colors.

Do not use the Sage Olive logo on medium-toned backgrounds where it disappears.

Design Principles

Whitespace

Let the work breathe. Generous margins and padding create a gallery-like experience.

Contrast

Bold black against soft cream. Delicate serif against rounded sans-serif. Tension creates interest.

Texture

Subtle grain, natural paper textures, and organic shapes add depth and authenticity.

Restraint

Less is more. Every element must earn its place. Remove until only the essential remains.

Tone of Voice

Sophisticated

Speak with quiet confidence. Avoid exclamation marks and hyperbole. Let the work speak louder than the words.

"A morning ceremony in Ubud, where incense met the first light and two souls became one."

Artistic

Use evocative, sensory language. Paint pictures with words. Reference light, texture, emotion, and place.

"We found them at golden hour, barefoot on volcanic sand, the ocean writing their story in foam."

Professional

Be clear and direct in business communications. Proposals and emails should be warm but efficient.

"Your gallery of 300+ edited images will be delivered within 21 working days via a private online gallery."

Storytelling

Every caption, every description should tell a micro-story. Focus on the "why" behind the moment, not just the "what."

"She didn't know he was watching. That's when we pressed the shutter — in the space between awareness and abandon."
Language Guidelines

Primary Language

All client-facing communications in English. Use British English spelling for international appeal (colour, favourite, centre).

  • Website copy in English
  • Social media captions in English
  • Proposals and contracts in English
  • Occasional Bahasa Indonesia for local context

Words to Use / Avoid

Use: Capture, reveal, craft, soul, light, story, moment, authentic, intimate, timeless

Avoid: Cheap, discount, snap, pic, selfie, package deal, basic, standard, budget

06 — Photography Style

Through the Lens

Our photography style is the heart of the brand. Warm, emotive, and deeply human — every image should feel like a memory you can almost touch.

Warm Tones

Rich, golden warmth. Skin tones glow. Shadows lean amber, not blue. The island's golden hour lives in every frame.

Deep Shadows

Embrace darkness. Shadows add mystery and depth. Not crushed — rich. There is detail in the dark.

Natural Texture

Focus on skin, fabric, stone, water, leaves. Macro details that make the viewer feel the surface.

Color Grading Reference

Warm Highlight → Mid Tone → Rich Shadow → Deep Black

Photography Guidelines

Composition

  • Prioritize negative space — let subjects breathe
  • Use natural framing: doorways, foliage, architecture
  • Shoot at eye level or slightly below for intimacy
  • Include environmental context (Bali landscapes, temples, ocean)
  • Candid over posed — capture the in-between moments

Post-Processing

  • Warm white balance (5800K–6500K range)
  • Slightly desaturated — never oversaturated
  • Lift blacks slightly for a filmic quality
  • Add subtle grain for texture (15–25% in Lightroom)
  • Consistent color grading across entire galleries
